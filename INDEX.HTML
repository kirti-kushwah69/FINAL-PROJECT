india is in the 21st century

india's most influential and iconic structures built in 21st century

India, a developing country has seen a lot of political and socio-cultural shifts in its landscape since the independence. Architecture in India in the 21st centuries has played a major role in nation-building and creating an identity globally. The architecture has changed the social, political, cultural, and economic context of time. The Republic of India is constantly evolving and growing, and so is its architecture. With the emerging technology and resources, India is today almost equal to or matching up to all the major developed countries in the world.

the statue of unity

The tallest statue in the world is one of the most iconic and influential buildings of 21st-century India. The Statue of Unity depicting former Indian Deputy Prime Minister Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was designed by Michael Graves Architecture and Design and stands 182 meters tall (almost twice the height of the Statue of Liberty in New York City). The statue required over 18,000 tonnes of reinforced steel and 1,700 tonnes of bronze. This architectural marvel is today India’s biggest victory worldwide.

New Building for the Parliament of India

The new building for the Parliament of India is the first purpose-designed Parliament building for India. The design addresses the present and future needs of efficient Parliamentary functioning. Designed by HCP Designs, is the most impactful and influential building of the 21st century. The building is located on a triangular plot therefore, a triangular building allows for the most efficient use of available space. The trinity of functions – Lok Sabha (the Lower House), Rajya Sabha (the Upper House), and Central Lounge – also work efficiently within the triangular plan. The design is inspired by the present Parliament building, the construction crafts of India, India’s national symbols, and the other buildings at Central Vista.

Matri Mandir, Pondicherry

The modern and most influential architectural structure that has a cultural, spiritual, social, communal, and sustainable impact on architecture is the Matrimandir in the Auroville village of Pondicherry. A space for yoga, peace, and the soul. It took 37 years to build since its foundation. The entire structure is in the form of a geodesic dome with twelve petals around it, where the exterior façade design has golden discs that reflect the sunlight to generate a radiating surface.

National War Memorial

The National War Memorial is constructed on the idea of the rebirth of those unsung heroes through their stories, journeys, and struggles communicated and expressed in a spatial tangible form. A culmination of the historical Rajpath extending through the India Gate, the National War Memorial is an open landscaped public space spread over 42 acres in the C -hexagon. Progressive acts of protection, sacrifice, bravery, and becoming immortal translate as a concentric arrangement in which the ‘Thyag Chakra’ holds the name of each fallen soldier who becomes another brick in the nation’s defensive wall.
